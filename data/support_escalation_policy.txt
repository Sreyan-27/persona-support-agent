TECHCORP CLOUD - SUPPORT ESCALATION POLICY
============================================

PURPOSE
This document defines when a support conversation must be handed off from automated or tier-1 support to a human specialist, and how that handoff should be packaged.

ALWAYS ESCALATE (NO EXCEPTIONS)
- Any billing dispute, refund request, or report of a duplicate/unexpected charge.
- Any request to disable or bypass account security controls (2FA, SSO) without going through identity verification.
- Any account flagged for a security lockout (suspicious activity), as opposed to a standard failed-login lockout.
- Legal requests: subpoenas, GDPR/CCPA data requests, terms-of-service disputes, contract negotiation.
- Enterprise contract or custom SLA term changes.
- Data restore requests (see data_export_and_backup.md) — restores are never self-service.
- 2FA recovery when all backup codes are exhausted and the authenticator device is lost.

ESCALATE WHEN UNRESOLVED
- The available documentation does not directly answer the customer's question.
- The customer indicates that previously suggested steps did not resolve the issue (repeated back-and-forth without progress).
- The customer's tone indicates significant frustration or repeated complaints across the conversation, even if the underlying issue is normally self-service.
- Retrieval confidence from the knowledge base is low for the query (no clearly relevant article found).

RESPONSE TIME EXPECTATIONS BY TIER (post-escalation)
| Plan | First Human Response |
|------|------------------------|
| Free | Community forum, best effort |
| Pro | 24 hours |
| Business | 8 hours |
| Enterprise | 1 hour, 24/7 |

HANDOFF PACKAGE REQUIREMENTS
Every escalation must be accompanied by a structured summary including: detected customer persona, a concise issue summary, conversation history, which knowledge base documents were consulted, what troubleshooting steps were already attempted, and a recommended next action for the human agent. This prevents the customer from having to repeat information already provided to the automated system.
