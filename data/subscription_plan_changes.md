# Subscription Plan Changes

## Available Plans
| Plan | Monthly Price | API Calls / mo | Storage | Support SLA |
|------|---------------|-----------------|---------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | 1,000 | 1 GB | Community forum only |
| Pro | $49 | 50,000 | 50 GB | Email, 24h response |
| Business | $199 | 250,000 | 500 GB | Email + chat, 8h response |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Custom | 24/7, 1h response, dedicated CSM |

## Upgrading
Upgrades take effect immediately. You are charged a prorated amount for the remainder of the current billing cycle, and the new plan's limits apply right away — no waiting for the next cycle.

## Downgrading
Downgrades take effect at the start of the next billing cycle, not immediately. This prevents customers from losing access to data or features mid-cycle that they already paid for. If your current usage exceeds the new plan's limits (e.g., storage), you will be notified and given 14 days to reduce usage before any data is archived.

## Switching Between Monthly and Annual Billing
Switching to annual billing can be done at any time and immediately applies the standard 2-month-free annual discount, prorated against the remaining monthly term. Switching from annual back to monthly is only possible at the end of the current annual term.

## Enterprise Custom Plans
Enterprise plans are negotiated directly with an account manager and include custom rate limits, dedicated infrastructure options, custom SLA terms, and a named Customer Success Manager. Changes to Enterprise contract terms (pricing, SLA, seat count) require account manager involvement and cannot be processed through self-service or this support agent.

## Business Impact of Plan Changes
- Upgrading: no downtime; new limits apply within seconds of confirmation.
- Downgrading: scheduled, non-disruptive; takes effect at next renewal.
- Enterprise contract changes: typically take 3–5 business days to finalize once terms are agreed with the account manager.
